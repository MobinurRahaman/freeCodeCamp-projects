A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Here's a time line of A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's life:

  • October 15, 1931: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam is born in Rameswaram, Madras Presidency, British India (now Tamil Nadu, India).
  • 1954: Graduates with a degree in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1955: Joins the Madras Institute of Technology as a lecturer.
  • 1960-1962: Earns a Master's degree in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Michigan and a Doctorate in Aerospace Engineering from the Carnegie Mellon University.
  • 1963-1982: Works as a scientist and engineer at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  • 1982-1992: Serves as the Director of the DRDO's Electronics and Aerospace Technology Division.
  • 1992-1999: Serves as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India.
  • 1999-2002: Serves as the 11th President of India.
  • July 27, 2015: Dies in Shillong, Meghalaya, India, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management.

APJ Abdul Kalam's speeches were a reflection of his deep understanding of the human psyche and his unwavering faith in the power of human potential. He had a remarkable ability to inspire people to overcome their limitations and reach for the stars.

- Nelson Mandela, Former President of South Africa
